Step 1 of 4: Nisab Threshold

Set your Nisab

Nisab is the minimum wealth threshold that makes Zakat obligatory. Choose which scholarly standard to apply.

⚠️ About these values: Nisab is calculated from live gold and silver spot prices (595g silver · 85g gold). The values shown are today's estimates. In production, connect to a live metals API to keep these in sync with nzf.org.au. NZF uses the same formula: Silver Nisab = 595 × AUD silver spot price.
📚What is Nisab and which should I use?▼

The Prophet ﷺ set the Nisab at 85g of gold or 595g of silver. Most contemporary scholars, including those endorsed by the Australian National Imams Council (ANIC), recommend the silver Nisab. Its lower threshold means more Muslims are included in the obligation to give, which is consistent with the purpose of Zakat.

Step 1 of 7: Nisab Threshold

Before you begin

Choose your Nisab standard. This is the only setting needed before entering your assets.

About your Zakat anniversary (hawl): Your hawl is the lunar year that must pass on wealth before Zakat becomes due. It starts from the date your wealth first exceeded the Nisab threshold. Once you know that date, return to it each year to calculate and pay your Zakat. If you are unsure of the exact date, many scholars accept Ramadan as a convenient annual default.
Business owners with multiple hawl dates: If your business was established after your personal savings first reached Nisab, your business assets may have a separate anniversary. Note the date each asset category was first acquired above Nisab and apply Zakat only to those that have completed a full lunar year.
⚖Nisab threshold▼
NZF recommends the Silver Nisab for mixed assets as it benefits more recipients. The Gold Nisab applies if you exclusively hold gold.

Step 5 of 7: Business Assets

Business assets

Zakat applies to liquid business assets and trading stock — not fixed assets like machinery, vehicles, or property used in the business.

Not zakatable: Fixed assets (equipment, vehicles, computers, office furniture, land, buildings), intellectual property, goodwill, and prepaid expenses for future use.
🏢Business structure▼
Sole trader
Business and personal wealth calculated together. Include business cash in your personal totals above.
Partnership
Each partner pays Zakat on their share of the zakatable business assets.
Company or trust
Zakat on your proportional share of the company's zakatable assets.

Step 6 of 7: Deductions

Deductions and liabilities

Only debts that are due right now can be deducted. Long-term loans like a mortgage cannot be deducted in full — only the current instalment may qualify.

Core rule: Only debts payable within this lunar month can be deducted. A 30-year mortgage cannot be deducted in full. Only this month's repayment instalment qualifies.
